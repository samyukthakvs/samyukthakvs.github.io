What's Broken in Their World

Three pains that open every deal.

Before you pitch anything, make sure they feel seen. These are the problems keeping your buyer up at night — use them to open the conversation.

⚠️

Compliance is Always a Fire Drill

A Labour Inspector walks in. Forms 13–23 are incomplete. Three contractors haven't filed PF challans. The Plant HR manager spends a full day pulling together what should be instant.

"It would take at least a day — and they still wouldn't all be complete."
💸

Payroll Leakage Nobody's Audited

Ghost workers on the biometric roll. Night shift pay going to day workers. OT manipulation no one's caught. Finance has never verified whether attendance actually matches payroll — because there's been no way to.

"OT is a big line item, but we've never audited the link to attendance."
📊

The CHRO Is Flying Blind

Attrition from Pune. Headcount from Manesar. Compliance from Chennai. All arriving as separate Excel files on different days. By the time it's consolidated for the board, it's already stale.

"It takes 3–4 days to pull together. It's never fully accurate."

When to Move

Spot the trigger. Know what to say.

Manufacturing deals are event-driven. These are the moments to act — and the opening line for each one.

🔴 ACT NOW · 0–4 weeks

Labour Inspector Visit or PF Audit

They're in crisis mode. Call the CHRO directly. Say: "We can have your registers audit-ready in 30 days — here's how we did it for [client]."

Close window: 0–4 weeks
🔴 ACT NOW · 0–6 weeks

Worker Walkout or Payroll Fraud Flagged

Don't pitch the product. Lead with risk. Say: "Every payroll error is an IR risk — we eliminate the root cause, not just the symptom."

Close window: 0–6 weeks
🔴 ACT NOW · 2–8 weeks

State Labour Code Notification

Paper registers are now a legal liability. Say: "We auto-generate every required digital register — any inspector, any time, any plant."

Close window: 2–8 weeks
🟡 PRIORITISE · 4–12 weeks

New CHRO Just Joined

First 60 days = the highest buying window. Reach out within 2 weeks. Help them shape the agenda before it's already set.

Close window: 4–12 weeks
🟡 PRIORITISE · 6–16 weeks

PE Investment or IPO Filing

New investors will immediately ask for workforce data. Say: "Here's how to be board-ready with real-time HR metrics — before your 100-day review."

Close window: 6–16 weeks
🟢 NURTURE · 12–24 weeks

Crossed 5,000 Headcount or Digital Mandate

Excel is visibly breaking. CEO has announced Industry 4.0. Engage now, position PeopleStrong as the HR data foundation — before they pick the wrong architecture.

Close window: 12–24 weeks

In the Meeting

Six questions. Use them verbatim.

Each question is engineered to surface a specific pain. Let the buyer hear themselves say it out loud — that's where the deal starts.

🔴 CHRO — Board Pressure

"When your MD or board asks for a workforce snapshot across all plants — what does that process look like today? How much of it is Excel?"

🟠 Plant HR — Monday Morning

"Walk me through your Monday morning — from when you arrive, to when you send the manpower report to Production. What does that look like?"

🟠 Plant HR — Inspector Readiness

"When a Labour Inspector walks in unannounced, how long does it take you to pull together all the CLRA registers for your contractors?"

🟡 CFO — OT Leakage

"Do you have a sense of what overtime spend looks like as a percentage of your wage bill? Has it ever been audited for accuracy?"

🔵 Plant Head / COO — Production Impact

"How often does a production line get delayed because of a manpower shortage you didn't see coming? What does that cost per hour?"

🔴 ALL — The Golden Question

"If I asked your Plant HR Manager right now how ready they are for an unannounced Labour Inspector visit — what do you think they'd say?"
